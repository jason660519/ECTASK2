Master’s Report Formatting Specification

1. Document Layout & Geometry

- Page size: A4 standard.
- Margins: 2.54 cm (1 inch) on all sides.
- Orientation: Portrait (unless appendices require landscape tables).
- Page numbering: Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3) starting on the Introduction page.
- Preliminary pages such as the Executive Summary and Table of Contents may use Roman numerals (i, ii, iii).
- Place page numbers in the bottom centre or bottom right.

2. Typography & Font Hierarchy

- Body font: Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman.
- Body font size: 11 pt for Arial/Calibri, or 12 pt for Times New Roman.
- Main headings (H1): 16 pt, bold, left-aligned.
- Subheadings (H2): 14 pt, bold, left-aligned.
- Sub-subheadings (H3): 12 pt, bold or italic, left-aligned.
- Captions (tables/figures): 10 pt, italic; captions go below figures and above tables.

3. Spacing & Alignment

- Line spacing: 1.5 lines for body text.
- Paragraph spacing: 6 pt or 8 pt "space after".
- Do not use double returns to create extra spacing.
- Text alignment: left-aligned is preferred, or justified if required.
- Bullet points: use single line spacing within each item, with a 6 pt space after the final bullet.

4. Tables, Figures, & Appendices

- Data presentation: center all tables and figures.
- Referencing: number every visual aid (e.g., Figure 1, Table 1) and cite it in the body text.
- Appendices: start each appendix on a new page labeled "Appendix A", "Appendix B", etc.
